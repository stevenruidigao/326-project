Overview

Our vision focuses on improving the learning experience of students across the world. Students possess a multitude of knowledge that can be exchanged, resulting in improved learning for everybody. Our web app aims to connect these varying pieces of knowledge. Specifically, we tailor to students who can help others with their classes in exchange for the same in return. Tutoring is often a one-way exchange of information in which only the tutee benefits. However, we want all parties involved to partake in learning and leave the session with knowledge that they deem useful and/or interesting. If you’re a student that needs help with CS 326 (just an example!), you can find a tutor that is willing to help but is looking to learn more about another area that you are familiar with. In that case, you’d both be a match and could tutor each other on the other’s desired subject. There’s no need for monetary exchange; the only exchange necessary is that of the mind.

Application Parts

Authentication is a primary component of our application. Since we want users (users are both tutors and tutees) to showcase what they can teach others and connect with them, they need a profile that contains that information such that other tutors can find them. Thus, a login is needed.
Profiles themselves are a critical component. For a user to be able to find someone who can teach them what they need to know, and to whom they can teach something in return, this information has to be available. Thus, profiles will contain a user’s “strengths” and “weaknesses” - or what they can teach others, and what they need to be taught/tutored on. To showcase the legitimacy of these users, there’ll be an XP system with ratings and comments that allows for others to provide feedback on their experience with said user.
Since the aim is to connect with someone so that both can expand their horizons, there also needs to be a method to communicate. Thus, a chat system that allows for inter-user communication is key. In addition to this chat, we’ll have a way to make requests – this will likely be how a chat gets opened between users. Then, they can communicate and establish what they can both gain from the swap.
